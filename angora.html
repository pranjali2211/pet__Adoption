ANGORA RABBIT

🐇Congratulations on adopting a 4-month-old Angora rabbit🐰!

✅ 1.Diet (extra important for Angoras)

🌾 Hay (MOST important)
  • Unlimited grass hay at all times
  • Timothy, orchard, or meadow hay
  • Angoras are high risk for wool block, so hay intake is critical
  • 🟤 Pellets
  • Alfalfa-based pellets (until ~6–7 months)
  • About ¼ cup per day (adjust for size)
  • Chooseplain pellets only (no seeds, corn, or dried fruit)
    • 🥬 Greens🥕
  • Introduce slowly if not already eating greens
  • About 1 packed cup per 2 lbs body weight per day
    • Good Greens🌿: Romaine lettuce, Cilantro, Basil, Parsley, Dandelion greens, Bok choy greens
    ❌ Avoid iceberg lettuce
    🍎Treats: Very limited, Tiny portions, 1–2x/week max,Apple (no seeds), strawberry, banana (tiny slice)

✅ 2.Grooming (ANGORA-SPECIFIC & CRITICAL)

    This is the biggest difference from other rabbits.

  • ✂️ Daily or Near-Daily Brushing
    • Minimum: every other day
  • Daily during shedding
  • Use:
  • Slicker brush
    • Wide-tooth comb
  • Gently check:
  • Belly
  • Behind ears
  • Armpits
  • Butt area (prone to mats)
  • ✂️ Wool Trimming
  • Every 2–3 months
  • Keeps fur manageable and prevents overheating

✅ 3.Housing & Environment🏠 Space

  • Minimum4x4 ftenclosure + daily exercise
  • Indoor living strongly recommended (heat-sensitive)
  • 🌡Temperature
  • Ideal:60–70°F (16–21°C)
  • Heat is dangerous for Angoras
  • Frozen water bottles wrapped in cloth during warm weather
  • 🛏 Flooring🧺
  • Soft, clean, non-slip
  • Keep fur dry and clean (wet fur mats instantly)

✅ 4.Litter Training

  • Most Angoras litter train well
  • Use:
  • Paper-based litter or wood pellets
  • Large litter box (their fluff needs space!)
  • Put hay in the box corner

✅ 5.🩺Health & Vet Care🐇

  • Exotic Vet

    • Essential—Angoras arenot beginner-vet rabbits

  • ✂️ Spay/Neuter
  • Around 5–6 months
  • Improves behavior and health
  • Makes grooming easier (less stress)

✅ 6.❤️ Handling & Bondinge

  • Handle close to the floor
  • Support hind legs
  • Many Angoras enjoy calm brushing once bonded
  • Talk softly—slow movements
