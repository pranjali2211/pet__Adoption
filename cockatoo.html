1️⃣.🏠Cage Setup🐦

  • Large cage Cockatoos need space to stretch wings (min 90×90×120 cm).
  • perches Multiple perches of different sizes & textures.
  • Toys Bells, swings, chew toys (mental stimulation).
    • Place the Cage:
  • Placement Bright room with natural light, away from drafts and kitchen smoke.

2️⃣.Diet🥗

  • Pallets: High-quality Cockatoo pellets daily.
  • Vegetables Carrot, broccoli, spinach.
  • Small fruit pices:apple,banana occasionally (Avoid Avacado)
    • Extras :Fresh water daily, cuttlebone or mineral block for calcium.
    Cuttlebonw or mineral block for calcium
    Avoid :Chocolate, onion, garlic, salty/sugary food.

3️⃣.Exersice & Play🕊️

  • Daily free time: 1–2 hours outside the cage (supervised).
  • Toys: Rotate weekly to prevent boredom.
  • Flight / climbing: Provide safe space to climb, swing, and explore.

4️⃣.Cage Cleaning🧹

  • Daily: Replace cage paper, wash food & water bowls.
  • Weekly: Deep clean cage (soap + water, rinse well).
  • Health benefit: Clean environment reduces risk of infections.

5️⃣.🩺Health Care

    Watch for signs of illness:

  • Fluffed feathers constantly
  • loss of appetite
  • Lethargy
  • Changes in droppings
    • if you see these signs,consult anavian veterinarian.

6️⃣.Bonding & Socialization🤝

    Cockatoos are highly social and affectionate.

    Tips:
  • Talk softly, offer treats by hand.
  • Let the bird sit on your finger or shoulder.
  • Spend daily time near the cage.
    • Mental health: Play, interaction, and social bonding reduce screaming and stress.

7️⃣.Sleep🌙

    Hours needed 10–12 hours of uninterrupted sleep.

    Tips:
  • Quiet, dark area at night.
  • Cover cage lightly if needed.
