1️⃣.🏠Cage Setup🦦

  • Large cage: Minimum 36×24×24 inches, multi-level preferred.
  • Bedding : Multiple perches of different sizes & textures.
  • Hammocks & tunnels: Ferrets love climbing and hiding.
  • Food & water bowls: Heavy ceramic or attached bowls to prevent tipping.
  • Litter box: Corner litter box with paper-based or pelleted litter.
  • Placement: Away from direct sunlight and drafts, in a quiet, secure room.

2️⃣.Diet🍖

  • High protein & fat diet: Ferrets are obligate carnivores.
  • Commercial ferret kibble: 30–40% protein, 15–20% fat.
  • Raw meat or cooked meats: Chicken, turkey, small amounts of liver occasionally.
  • Treats: Small freeze-dried meats, ferret-specific treats.
  • Avoid: Sugary foods, fruits, vegetables, dairy, or grains (ferrets cannot digest them).
    • Cuttlebonw or mineral block for calcium
  • Water: Fresh water available at all times (bottle or bowl).

3️⃣.Grooming & Hygiene🛁

  • Bathing: Only when necessary (ferrets have natural oils; too much bathing dries skin).
  • Toys: Tubes, tunnels, balls, and interactive toys.
  • Social play: Ferrets are social; play with your ferret for mental stimulation.
  • Nail Care: Trimming: Every 2–3 weeks. Overgrown nails can scratch you or get caught..

4️⃣.Exersice & Play🏃

  • Out-of-cage time: At least 2–4 hours daily in a ferret-proofed room.
  • Toys: Tubes, tunnels, balls, and interactive toys.
  • Social play: Ferrets are social; play with your ferret for mental stimulation.
  • Safety: Ensure no small gaps, wires, or items they can chew/swallow.

5️⃣.Cage Cleaning🧹

  • Daily: Replace cage paper, wash food & water bowls.
  • Weekly: Deep clean cage (soap + water, rinse well).
  • Health benefit: Clean environment reduces risk of infections.

6️⃣.🩺Health Care

  • Annual vet checkups: Vaccinations (rabies, distemper), parasite check.
    • Watch for signs of illness:
  • loss of appetite
  • Lethargy
  • Diarrhea or vomiting
  • Coughing, sneezing, or discharge
    • Spay/Neuter: Recommended if not already done to prevent health and behavioral issues.

7️⃣.Bonding & Socialization🤝

    Ferrets are highly social and affectionate.

    Tips for Bonding🤝:
  • Daily play and interaction.
  • Gentle handling; support the body fully.
  • Let them explore safe areas while supervised.
    • Mental enrichment: Treat puzzles, tunnels, and toys reduce boredom.

8️⃣.Sleep🌙

    Ferrets sleep 18+ hours per day.

  • Provide cozy hammocks, tunnels, or beds.
  • Make sure their sleeping area is quiet and safe.
